What is AAB?

The AI Assessment Board (AAB) is a nonprofit initiative focused on documenting and supporting understanding of AI capabilities in educational contexts.

Our work is independent, transparent, and public-interest driven.

Read More

Case Registry

A curated collection of verified AI education cases from real-world settings. Cases are collected and reviewed from university faculty, researchers, and experienced AI-education practitioners through documented research projects and instructional programs.

Pilot Registry

A record of structured AI education pilots conducted with institutional and practitioner partners.

Our Process

From Innovation to Standardization: The AAB Case & Pilot Registry provides structured, de-identified documentation of real-world AI education practices that researchers can cite to study design patterns, adoption contexts, and emerging standards—without functioning as a human-subjects research dataset

Our Principles

Independence

AAB operates independently of commercial, political, or institutional control.

Neutrality

Documentation does not favor specific tools, platforms, or providers.

Transparency

Methods, assumptions, and limitations are made explicit.

Public Interest

We prioritize shared understanding over private advantage.
